Program Overview

For successful completion of the degree, all students must satisfy the general education requirements explained in the UMBC Undergraduate Catalog. All Information Systems majors must complete at least 9 upper level IS and Management courses taught by the IS department at UMBC.

Important Notes & Requirements

Minimum Grade Requirements:
Students must earn a grade of "C" or better for any course to be applied to the Information Systems major.

Gateway Requirements:
Before being allowed to take IS 410 and 450 (and then the rest of the IS 400-level core courses), students must first complete the following:

  • A grade of "C" or higher in:
    • MATH 155 – Elementary Calculus
    • IS 147 – Introduction to Computer Programming
  • A grade of "B" or higher in:
    • IS 300 – Management Information Systems
    • IS 310 – Software and Hardware Concepts

Residency Requirement:
IS 300 and IS 310 must be completed in residence. Information Systems does not support third time repeat petitions for the purposes of continuing in this major.

Upper-Level Electives:
Examples of upper-level electives include: IS 303, IS 304, IS 460, IS 461, IS 427, IS 428, IS 430, IS 438, IS 464, IS 471, IS 472, IS 451U, IS 451M, and IS 452. See the catalog for a full listing.

Special Courses:
Courses including IS 399, IS 478, IS 479, IS 469, IS 397, and IS 467 cannot be used to satisfy the IS upper-level elective requirement, but students can use them to gain academic credit for internships, co-ops, and research projects. See your advisor for more information.
