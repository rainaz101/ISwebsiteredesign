Bachelor of Science in Information Systems

Comprehensive curriculum designed for IS professionals

Program Requirements

For successful completion of the degree, all students must satisfy the general education requirements. All Information Systems majors must complete at least 9 upper level IS and Management courses taught by the IS department at UMBC. Students must earn a grade of "C" or better for any course to be applied to the Information Systems major.

Required Courses

  • IS 300 – Management Information Systems
  • IS 310 – Software and Hardware Concepts
  • IS 410 – Database Design

Core IS Courses

  • IS 420 – Database Application Development
  • IS 425 – Decision Support Systems
  • IS 436 – Systems Analysis & Design

Mathematics & CS

  • MATH 155 – Applied Calculus
  • MATH 215 – Finite Mathematics
  • STAT 351 – Applied Statistics
  • IS 147/CMSC 201 – Computer Programming

Network Courses

  • IS 450 – Data Communications and Networks
  • IS 451 – Network Design and Management
  • Upper Level IS Elective

Administrative Science Requirements

MGMT 210, ECON 101, ECON 102, ECON 121, ECON 122, and Technical Communication

Gateway Requirements

Before taking IS 410 and 450: Grade of "C" or higher in MATH 155 & IS 147, and "B" or higher in IS 300 & IS 310
